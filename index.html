A. Total Scholarly Output (2019–2025)

Category: Productivity
What it measures: Total number of publications per university per year (2019–2025).
Why it’s important: Baseline capacity and growth trend; shows expansion or contraction of research footprint.
How to calculate / formula: Count publications by year from Dataset 2, grouped by institution; build yearly time series.
Strategic value: Essential for growth trajectory and peer scale comparisons.
file: A_01_A. Total Scholarly Output _2019_2025_.html

B. Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) in Publications

Category: Productivity
What it measures: Average annual growth rate of publications across the 2019→2025 period.
Why it’s important: Signals sustained momentum versus volatility.
How to calculate / formula: CAGR = (Ending/Beginning)^(1/years) − 1. Example: [(Pubs2025 / Pubs2019)^(1/6)] − 1.
Strategic value: Assess sustainability; >5% strong, <0% decline.
Note: CAGR in publications (2019→2025).
file: C_01_B. CAGR of Publications.html

C. Year-over-Year (YoY) Growth Rate

Category: Productivity
What it measures: Percent change in publications from one year to the next.
Why it’s important: Detects volatility and inflection points masked by CAGR.
How to calculate / formula: YoY = (Pubs_N − Pubs_{N−1}) / Pubs_{N−1} × 100.
Strategic value: Explains peaks/valleys by year (funding, policy, pandemic).
file: D_01_C. YoY Growth.html

D. Publications per Active Author

Category: Productivity
What it measures: Average productivity per researcher per year.
Why it’s important: Separates growth from headcount vs. individual output; flags efficiency/mentorship needs.
How to calculate / formula: Using Dataset 2, count unique authors per year; divide total publications by active authors.
Strategic value: Low ratio → support/mentorship; very high → check quality balance.
file: F_01_D. Publications per Active Author.html

E. Publication Distribution by Field (ASJC/QS/THE)

Category: Field Productivity
What it measures: Number and share of publications by research area (ASJC/QS/THE taxonomies).
Why it’s important: Reveals research profile and strategic focus areas.
How to calculate / formula: Group by field; compute counts and percentages by year and by university.
Strategic value: Identify strengths, gaps, and niches vs. peers.
Note: Publication Distribution by Field (ASJC).
file: G_01_E. Field Mix USFQ _2025_.html

H. Field Concentration Index (HHI)

Category: Field Productivity
What it measures: Degree of diversification vs. concentration across fields.
Why it’s important: Balanced portfolios reduce risk; concentration limits opportunities.
How to calculate / formula: HHI = sum of squared field shares; near 1 = concentrated; near 0 = diversified.
Strategic value: Guide to diversify or double down on core strengths.
file: L_01_H. HHI by field _2025_.html

I. Field-Weighted Citation Impact (FWCI) — Average

Category: Impact
What it measures: Citations relative to the global average in the same field and year.
Why it’s important: Primary quality metric; normalizes for field and age.
How to calculate / formula: Average FWCI across publications per university/year; 1.0 = world avg; >1.0 above average.
Strategic value: Direct measure of quality and competitiveness.
Note: Field-Weighted Citation Impact (FWCI) — Institutional Average.
file: M_01_I. Average FWCI.html

J. FWCI by Field

Category: Impact
What it measures: Citation quality within specific disciplines.
Why it’s important: Strengths and weaknesses vary by field.
How to calculate / formula: Group by field; compute mean FWCI; compare across universities and over time.
Strategic value: Targets investments and improvements by field.
Note: FWCI by Field (latest year).
file: O_01_J. USFQ FWCI by Field _2025_.html

K. Citations per Publication

Category: Impact
What it measures: Total citations divided by number of publications.
Why it’s important: Simple benchmark; complements FWCI.
How to calculate / formula: Sum citations by university/year/field; divide by publications; consider age effects.
Strategic value: Easy for non-technical audiences (use with year filters).
file: P_01_K. Citations per Publication.html

L. Outputs in Top Citation Percentiles

Category: Impact
What it measures: Share of outputs in top 1%, 5%, 10%, 25% most-cited globally.
Why it’s important: Indicates breakthrough, high-impact research.
How to calculate / formula: Use 'Outputs in Top Citation Percentiles' from Dataset 2; compute % per band.
Strategic value: Excellence indicator (Top 10% >15% is a strong target).
Note: Outputs in Top Citation Percentiles (Top 10%) — proxy.
file: R_01_L. _ Outputs in Top 10_ _proxy_.html

M. h-index (Institution / Authors)

Category: Impact
What it measures: h papers with ≥ h citations at institution or author level.
Why it’s important: Balances productivity and impact (long-career bias).
How to calculate / formula: Institutional: rank all papers by citations, find h ≈ rank = citations; Author: from Dataset 1.
Strategic value: Benchmark leaders; track change over time.
Note: h-index (Institutional, 2019–2025 window).
file: S_01_M. Institutional h-index _window_.html

AH. Relative Growth Index

Category: Strategic Positioning
What it measures: USFQ growth vs. peer average growth.
Why it’s important: Contextualizes absolute growth.
How to calculate / formula: RGI = (USFQ CAGR) / (Avg CAGR of other 5). RGI>1 outpacing peers.
Strategic value: Detects if pace is ahead or behind.
Note: Relative Growth Index (RGI).
file: Z_09_AH. Relative Growth Index.html

AI. Quality-Adjusted Output

Category: Strategic Positioning
What it measures: Volume weighted by impact.
Why it’s important: Fewer high-impact papers may beat more low-impact ones.
How to calculate / formula: Sum(Publication_i × FWCI_i) or Pubs × Avg FWCI.
Strategic value: Balances quantity and quality.
Note: Quality-Adjusted Output (QAO).
file: Z_10_AI. Quality-Adjusted Output.html
